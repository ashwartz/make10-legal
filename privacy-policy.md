# Privacy Policy — Make10 (N10 — Number Bonds)

**Effective date:** 20 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Avi Shwartz ("we," "us," "our") handles
information in the mobile game **Make10 (N10 — Number Bonds)** (the "App"),
available on Google Play.

Make10 is built to be privacy-friendly and safe for all ages. **The App works
fully offline. We do not collect, transmit, or store any personal information
about you. We show no ads, use no analytics or tracking, and we do not sell or
share any data.**

## 1. The short version
- Everything happens on your device. Your high scores, progress, and settings are
  saved **only on your device**.
- We collect **no personal information** — no name, email, phone, location,
  contacts, or identifiers.
- We do not track you, and we do not send any data off your device.

## 2. Information stored on your device
The App saves your high scores, personal best, game progress, and settings (such
as sound and difficulty) **locally on your device only**. This information is
never sent to us or to anyone else. You can erase it at any time by clearing the
App's data in your device settings, or by uninstalling the App.

## 3. We do NOT
- Collect or transmit any personal information.
- Show advertising.
- Use analytics, tracking, or advertising SDKs.
- Access your location, contacts, camera, microphone, or files.
- Sell, rent, or share any data.

## 4. Children's privacy
Make10 is suitable for children and may be used by them. Because the App collects
no personal information from anyone, it collects none from children. We comply
with applicable children's privacy laws (including COPPA) and Google Play's
Families policy, and we never use data for advertising or profiling.

## 5. Your data and how to remove it
All app data lives on your device. To remove it, clear the App's storage in your
device settings, or uninstall the App.

## 6. Changes to this policy
We may update this Policy — for example, if we add online features such as a
leaderboard in a future version. We will change the "Effective date" above, post
the updated version at this URL, and describe any new data practices before they
take effect.
